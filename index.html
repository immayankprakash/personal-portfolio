  • Air Pollution Forecasting

    This project aims to forecast air pollution levels using advanced neural networks, specifically LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory) and GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit) models. These models are well suited for time series forecasting as they are able to capture and retain long-term dependencies in the data. By utilizing these models, the goal is to make accurate predictions of air pollution levels in a given location, helping decision-makers take appropriate actions and ultimately reducing negative impacts on human health and the environment.

  • Classifying DNA Sequence

    I am conducting a classification analysis of a gene family using DNA sequence data. The approach involves utilizing a k-mer counting technique to extract features from the DNA sequences, and then utilizing multiple machine learning models, including Logistic Regression, Multinomial Naive Bayes, and Neural Networks, to predict the gene family membership of the sequences. This multi-model approach allows for robust and accurate classification, while also providing the ability to compare and contrast the performance of the different models.
